Accessible Event Planning Guide - City & County of San Francisco

Section 1: Advertising the Event

  • Include descriptions of physical and programmatic accessibility features.
  • Provide contact information for requesting accommodations (e.g., ASL interpreters, captioning, alternate formats).
  • Include maps showing accessible features such as restrooms, parking, and seating.
  • Provide directions via accessible public transit, if applicable.
  • Ensure notices are screen reader compatible (use Word documents, accessible PDFs, or include text in the email body).
  • Use alternative text for images, graphics, and maps.

Section 2: Choosing an Accessible Site

  • Ensure an accessible route from street to entrance (firm, stable, slip-resistant, 36-48 inches wide).
  • Main entrance should be accessible or clearly marked with signage if alternate.
  • Interior path to meeting rooms and restrooms must be accessible.
  • Provide accessible loading zones and routes from transit stops.
  • If parking is provided, ensure at least 5 percent of spaces are accessible and 17 percent of those are van-accessible.
  • Provide accessible restrooms and amenities such as drinking fountains and telephones.

Section 3: Creating an Accessible Setup

  • Maintain 36 inches minimum circulation space (48-60 inches preferred).
  • Provide at least 5 percent wheelchair accessible seating with companion seats.
  • Disperse accessible seating throughout the venue.
  • Reserve seating near interpreters or captioning screens.
  • Ensure microphones and podiums are accessible.
  • Ensure displays and exhibits are reachable and do not protrude into walkways.
  • Provide food and beverage service on accessible routes with reachable dispensers.

Section 4: Providing Effective Communication

  • Offer materials in large print, Braille, audio, or electronic formats.
  • Caption all videos used in presentations.
  • For 50 or more attendees: Provide Assistive Listening Devices (ALDs) with signage.
  • For 100 or more attendees: Real-Time Captioning recommended.
  • For 500 or more attendees: ASL Interpreter recommended.
  • Provide oral descriptions of visual materials.
  • Offer speakerphone or audio conferencing options.
  • Include contact info in public notices for accommodation requests.
  • Make good faith efforts to fulfill late accommodation requests.

Section 5: Planning for Large Crowds

  • Ensure barriers do not obstruct accessible routes.
  • Maintain a 48 inches wide accessible path around work areas.
  • Keep circulation routes free of protruding objects.
  • Provide safe detours with signage and staff assistance if paths are blocked.
  • Ensure stages and platforms are accessible via ramp, lift, or portable lift.
  • Provide podiums no higher than 34 inches on accessible routes.

Section 6: Resources

  • Consult your Department's ADA Coordinator.
  • Visit the ADA Coordinator Contact List: http://sfgov.org/mod/ada-coordinators
  • Explore the Mayor's Office on Disability (MOD) website for resources.
  • Contact MOD at (415) 554-6789 or mod@sfgov.org
